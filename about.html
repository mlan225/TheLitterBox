About Us

Changing The Way You Communicate!

Our application, the Litter Box, is a somewhat anonymous, open chat tool that allows University of Kentucky students discuss and talk about specific classes, such as ICT 205, as well as specific sections (if the course has multiple sections/multiple instructors).

Who exactly is this app for?

Our app is mainly focused on UK undergraduate students that are wanting to discuss certain classes in an anonymous style of posting in a fashion that is similar to apps such as YikYak or reddit. The app would allow certain sections of classes to be able to communicate freely with one another and be able to ask questions such as: requests for tutoring, help on an assignment, etc. without having to go through the hassle of remembering student names or complicated student emails. It would also give a chance for students that are no longer in those classes to provide insights on how to do well in the class or how the professor acts/grades.

Our Goals and Aims?

Our ultimate goal is to be able to connect students in a way that allows for both present and past students to talk about specific classes with one another. Instead of only being able to talk to your fellow/current classmates, like the Canvas messenger, you would be able to talk to any previous student or students that are inquiring about the class for future reference. Anyone and everyone could contribute to the conversation.

What exactly does this app do?

Our app is simple: Users will be able to post discussion boards in a forum type style in the specific class and section, i.e. ICT 205 001, and ask questions about the class itself, assignments, the instructor, and everything inbetween. Users will be able to sign up with their UK student email account and create an “anonymous” user name and comment on other posts and make their own. Only those with a valid UK student email will be able to make an account.

Features and Functionality

  • Push notifications on followed posts or posts that the user is the original poster of
  • Log in to your own personal account that allows you to look at your post history, comments, and interactions
  • Be able to send direct messages to a selected user or a set of users
  • Forum threads can be hidden or reported for obscene, graphic, or irrelevant material
  • A voting system allows important or top rated posts to be at the beginning of the forum thread - Can be filtered (i.e. newest posts first, most popular posts first, etc.)

Graphic design and user interface

Our design is based on the classic forum type site (i.e. reddit, NeoGaf, etc.) where users can post text based discussions and engage with students of classes that they are either in, interested in taking in the future, or have currently been in. The simplistic design and straightforward user interface would allow a lot of content to be showing/present without having issues with information overload (i.e. having a feature that allows for posts to be minimized and comments in threads to be minimized after reading)
